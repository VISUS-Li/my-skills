# Motion, Editing, and VFX

Use this when designing animation, transitions, rhythm, captions, sound design, or HyperFrames segment prompts.

## Motion principles for agents

- Use anticipation: a small pre-move before a major reveal.
- Use slow-in/slow-out: objects rarely move linearly unless mechanical or glitchy.
- Use follow-through: labels, particles, shadows, or tails settle after the main object.
- Use staging: the audience must know where to look before an important event.
- Use exaggeration in short-video emphasis, but keep premium/enterprise styles restrained.

## Timing defaults

- Micro interaction: 0.18-0.35s.
- Label or icon entrance: 0.35-0.6s.
- Data/chart reveal: 0.8-1.6s.
- Scene phrase: 2-4s.
- Hook rhythm: faster cuts, 1-2.5s per shot.
- Explainer rhythm: 2.5-5s per shot.
- Summary rhythm: cleaner, slower, fewer competing elements.

## Transition families

Use transitions that match content:

- blur crossfade: calm scene-to-scene continuity.
- whip pan: contrast, speed, energy.
- mask wipe: product, editorial, premium reveal.
- data morph: chart/table/stat-to-claim transitions.
- light leak: aspirational/product/brand moments.
- ink wipe: guofeng/history.
- glitch: tech conflict, failure, cyber, but use sparingly.

Only use final fade-out at the end unless there is a narrative reason.

## Edit rhythm

Plan rhythm by act:

- Act 1: high curiosity, quick alternation, bold visual hook.
- Act 2: structured clarity, repeated visual grammar, proof details.
- Act 3: convergence, fewer elements, stronger CTA.

## Sound design notes

Even if the agent cannot generate final sound, plan it:

- whoosh for camera/scene moves.
- click/tick for UI/data steps.
- soft riser before reveal.
- bass hit for major claim.
- ambient bed matching style recipe.

Record assets and rights in `assets/asset_manifest.csv`.
